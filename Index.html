Why in need of a smart system to predict the quality of groundwater? majority of Sri Lankans rely themselves in it

Considering people of Sri Lanka, a major importance must be given to the water quality of the consuming water since a majority of the population directly depends on groundwater sources like wells and tube wells. According to the statistical report by Department of Census and Statistics, 40% of the entire Sri Lankan population has organized water supply facilities and 59.4 % is depending on other water sources such as rivers and ground water sources. However, the water supplied by the water supplying chains is not proven to be a quality water....

Majority of people does not prefer water supplied through supply chins due to the presence of high chlorine level which is proven not safe for infants and babies. In the data gathering survey, it is proven that in Sri Lanka, the water quality assessments being conducted more like a surface level evaluation. Instead of using water Quality Indexes (WQI) to precisely calculate the quality of water, the chains are only using a reference range of the particular parameters which indeed makes the identification process a weak one.

Apart from the quality of supplied water, people who use the water for consumption and domestic purposes must possess a clear idea on the quality of water they are dealing with. In a research conducted in Jaffna, it was found out that people tend to use water from natural sources directly and without taking any precautions and pretreatments. Out of 200 participants who participated, 87% used unsafe water for drinking and domestic purposes. Also, 57% had poor knowledge on the transmission and prevention of water borne diseases.

A smart system to identify the quality of groundwater which people are using on a daily basis is a vital need to avoid circumstances which are non advantageous and it is an important duty of youths to set path to a bright and healthy future where the society is ensured to lead a better lifestyle.
